Disco Elysium is a game about restoring the identity of a wreck of a character (the game's own word) who's lost his badge and his gun.
<br/>
It moves forward around the story of solving a case.<br/>
But are we actually solving anything?<br/>

Just getting to the body took what felt like forever.<br/>
The body is the single most important clue in a case — which made it all the stranger.<br/>
I met a string of characters first and worked through countless dialogue choices.<br/>
In a conversation with Kuno, the foul-mouthed kid loitering by the corpse, my morale ran out and I died.<br/>
Does it even make sense that a cop dies from low morale? You never know when it'll drop, so you have to buy supplements to top it back up.;;
<br/>
I picked options by the character's inner voices, but the case barely moved.<br/>
Less about the case, more about which action I chose — dying and starting over, again and again.<br/>
The harder I tried to solve the case, the more easily I died.
<br/>
Am I… actually solving this case?<br/>
The inner voices don't help you solve it; they make you choose how to act.<br/>
This isn't the structure of solving a case.<br/>
<br/>
This game has no clear right answer and no certain path to resolution.<br/>
It looks like a game about solving a case,<br/>
but it's really a game about deciding how to behave.
<br/>
When you finish, what you solved stays blurry.<br/>
The case remained as it was; only my choices were left.<br/>
What changed wasn't the case — only the actions I took to keep from dying.
